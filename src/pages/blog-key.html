Keeping your password safe

As the majority of people use technology for their daily transactions, we intend to secure our accounts with easy or random passwords. Oftentimes, we use personal information as a password so that we can easily remember it. However, we sometimes forget that this is the most important aspect of using our technology.


Hackers are mostly looking for your personal information, your first name, last name, and even the name of your pet. With this kind of password, they can easily gain access to your account. Once inside your account, they can do pretty much anything they want. Keep your password safe and reliable so as not to get into a situation like that.


Ways to keep your password safe

    The following list provides guidelines for creating moderate to strong passwords:

  • At least, 8 characters or more

  • Both uppercase and lowercase

  • Should be a mixture of numbers and letters

  • Special characters like “!@#$%^&*”

Make sure to randomize the order when creating a password. Not only can you start with letters, but you can also start with a special character or a number.


The use of 2FA

Some applications require us to set up two-factor authentication or what we call 2FA. Utilize this tool as it will add additional security to your account. 2FA is an extra layer of verification to determine if you are the actual account owner. Some 2FA procedures send you a text message or notification that you need to verify in order to log in to your account.


Encryption key management

Remembering all your passwords to all of your accounts is a bit of a hassle sometimes. Using a key management tool might help, however, you need to keep in mind that these also are hackable software. Some of the best tools are Azure Key Vault, Doppler, AWS Key Management Service, and much more.


Additional Note

If you feel that your account is not secured or there are malicious activities. Don’t hesitate to change your password following the above guidelines.


“It’s better to be safe than sorry.”
