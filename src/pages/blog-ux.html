My day-to-day activities as a UX designer

  • Create Personas through user research and data.

  • Create low fidelity wireframes and conduct concept meetings - sketch.

  • Usability testing and returning to client feedback.

  • Looking for creative ways to solve UX problems.

  • Developing interaction models, high-fidelity wireframes, and prototypes around customer success.

  • Discussions with developer, graphic designer and leads to formulate the product.


Create Personas through user research and data

My usual method of meeting our clients when I have a new project is to travel there myself. Because of the current pandemic, I use Zoom and Teams instead. In our first meeting, we will discuss the application's goals and expectations. Based on the application standards, I will perform user research. Interviews and surveys are two ways to conduct user research. By doing so, you will be able to determine who will be using the application and you can create a flow for the application's interaction.


As an example from one of my projects, let me introduce you to Giles Watson, my first User persona.



Create low fidelity wireframes and conduct concept meetings - sketch

Being aware of what to expect with the application and who will use it. We are moving on to the concept meetings with the client and creating low-fidelity wireframes.


From my experience, concept meetings are very helpful. Each individual has the freedom to discuss their own ideas and formulate them into a single flow. During this meeting, I gained a clearer understanding of what the application will be expecting, like user logins, profile settings, sharing capabilities to social media sites, etc. I can now create low-fidelity wireframes as shown below if the flow and epics have been agreed upon.



Usability testing and returning to client feedback

After I have done the first sketch, I will ask a user to complete a task for me. By doing this type of review with users, I will be able to determine if the design that I created will fail or pass. In addition, I will be able to determine if the user can do their own tasks easily and sustainably.


It will give me a good idea of which areas to improve on and which should be returned to the conceptual design stages. As a key to opening the release of the application, this step is considered crucial when doing UX work.


Looking for creative ways to solve UX problems

I've added this in the middle of the process as some UX designers encounter what we call "Creative block". Having spent so much time gathering requirements and doing the initial sketches, you might want to pause and think about what suits your project well. In my experience, the most effective way to think is to shut down your computer and go for a walk in the park. Sometimes the cause of creative block is pressuring yourself to finish the task and give an all-out effort to solve the problem. Manage your time and you can finish your task efficiently.


Once your creativity strikes back, let's now create the high fidelity wireframe and prototype.


Developing interaction models, high-fidelity wireframes, and prototypes around customer success.

Now that everything is settled for the application A-Z flow, I can create the interaction model, high fidelity wireframe, and prototype. The interaction model will support all user actions throughout the application. This allows me to easily design high-fidelity wireframes and stick them all together to produce a working prototype.


I mostly create high-fidelity wireframes using Adobe XD, Sketch, and Figma. It is fine to create the prototype on the same tool that you used for wireframes.



Discussions with developer, graphic designer and leads to formulate the product.

Once the application specification has been finalized, I usually create a functional document that has to be presented to the developers and project leads. This document contains all the design specifications that the developers will need. During this meeting, we will discuss the technical feasibility of the application. I will demonstrate my prototype and discuss the functional document.


In my experience with SCRUM teams, Sprint planning and backlog grooming are a part of the process. In this way, all the epics required by the application will be distributed. We create all of the tickets attached to my functional document and design in JIRA, then assign estimates to each ticket and epic to be discussed with the product owner.


Once everything is settled and discussed with Product owners and Leads. We start the Sprint 1!
